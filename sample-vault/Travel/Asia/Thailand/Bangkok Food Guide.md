# Bangkok Food Guide

My ultimate guide to eating in Bangkok after 6 weeks there in 2021.

## Street Food Heaven

### Must-Try Dishes

**Pad Thai**
- Best: Thip Samai (near Khao San)
- Order: "Pad Thai Houng Glua" (wrapped in thin egg)
- Price: 50-100 baht ($1.50-$3)

**Som Tam (Papaya Salad)**
- Spicy, sweet, sour, salty - all at once
- Ask for "pet nit noi" (a little spicy) if you can't handle heat
- Perfect with sticky rice

**Khao Soi** (Curry Noodles)
- Northern Thai dish
- Best at: Khao Soi Lamduan Fa Ham
- Creamy coconut curry, crispy noodles on top

**Mango Sticky Rice**
- Simple but divine
- Only eat in mango season (March-June)
- Mae Varee is the best spot

**Boat Noodles**
- Tiny bowls, powerful flavor
- Eat 3-4 bowls for a meal
- Victory Monument area has the best vendors

## Street Food Areas

**Chinatown (Yaowarat Road)**
- Opens evening time
- Chaos + amazing food
- Try: T&K Seafood, Nai Mong Hoi Tod
- My favorite area for street food

**Khao San Road**
- Tourist trap but fun
- Good for scorpions and insects (if you dare)
- Overpriced but convenient

**Or Tor Kor Market**
- Cleanest market in Bangkok
- Higher prices but worth it for quality
- Great for fruit

**Chatuchak Weekend Market**
- Huge, overwhelming, amazing
- Coconut ice cream in coconut shell
- Get there early (8am) to avoid heat

## Restaurants

**Jay Fai** (Michelin-starred street food)
- Famous for crab omelette
- Expect 2-hour wait
- Expensive for street food (~1000 baht)
- Worth it once

**Bo.lan** (Fine Dining)
- Traditional Thai, elevated
- Set menu: 2500 baht
- Make reservation

**Err Urban Rustic Thai**
- Hip, casual, delicious
- Creative takes on classic dishes
- Affordable (300-400 baht per person)

**Supanniga Eating Room**
- Elegant Thai cuisine
- Great for dinner
- River view location

## Food Court Gems

**MBK Food Court**
- Coupon system (buy coupons, use for food)
- Cheap, air-conditioned
- Good for trying many dishes

**Terminal 21 Food Court**
- Airport-themed floors
- Great food court on top floor
- 50-100 baht meals

## Breakfast Spots

**Jok Prince** (Rice Porridge)
- Open 24/7
- Post-night out favorite
- Comforting, simple

**Patongo** (Thai Donuts)
- With sweetened condensed milk or pandan custard
- Morning street food staple

## Coffee Culture

**Rocket Coffeebar**
- Third wave coffee
- Espresso tonic is perfect for the heat
- Multiple locations

**Roots Coffee**
- Hip, minimalist
- Good for working remotely
- Great cold brew

**Featherstone Bistro**
- Brunch spot
- Aussie-style coffee
- Popular with expats

## Drinks

**Thai Iced Tea** - Sweet, creamy, orange
**Coconut Water** - Fresh from the coconut
**Singha Beer** - Local lager
**Chang Beer** - Cheaper, everywhere
**Mekhong** - Thai whiskey (careful!)

## Food Tips

1. **Street food is safe** - Look for busy stalls with high turnover
2. **Eat where locals eat** - If there's a line, join it
3. **Cash only** - Most street vendors don't take cards
4. **Don't be afraid of spice** - but start mild and work up
5. **Eat fruit** - Mangosteen, rambutan, durian (if brave)
6. **Try everything once** - Even if it looks weird
7. **Hydrate** - It's HOT. Drink lots of water

## Food Phrases (Thai)

- "Aroi" = Delicious
- "Pet nit noi" = A little spicy
- "Mai pet" = Not spicy
- "Kee baht?" = How much?
- "Bin duay" = Check please

## Cost
Average meal costs:
- Street food: 40-80 baht ($1-2)
- Casual restaurant: 200-300 baht ($6-9)
- Nice restaurant: 500-1000 baht ($15-30)
- Fine dining: 2000+ baht ($60+)

## Warning
You WILL gain weight. The food is too good. Worth it.

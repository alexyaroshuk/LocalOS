# Tokyo Trip - May 2023

## Trip Overview
2 weeks in Tokyo - my first time in Japan. Life-changing experience.

**Duration**: May 10-24, 2023
**Budget**: $3,500 (flights, hotel, food, activities)
**Accommodation**: Shibuya - capsule hotel first week, Airbnb in Shinjuku second week

## Highlights

### Must-See Places
1. **Senso-ji Temple** (Asakusa)
   - Stunning traditional architecture
   - Early morning visit (7am) to avoid crowds
   - Best photos at golden hour

2. **Shibuya Crossing**
   - Watched it rain a hundred times, never got old
   - Best view: Starbucks in Tsutaya building
   - [[Photography/Favorite Shots 2024|Got amazing long-exposure shot]]

3. **Meiji Shrine**
   - Peaceful oasis in the middle of the city
   - Saw a traditional wedding ceremony!
   - Free entry

4. **TeamLab Borderless**
   - Digital art museum
   - Absolutely mind-blowing
   - Book tickets weeks in advance!

### Food Experiences
- **Best ramen**: Ichiran (solo dining booths!)
- **Best sushi**: Sushi Dai at Toyosu Market (2-hour wait worth it)
- **Best convenience store meal**: 7-Eleven egg salad sandwich (don't judge)
- **Best coffee**: Bear Pond Espresso in Shimokitazawa
- **Unexpected favorite**: Conveyor belt sushi (so fun!)

### Neighborhoods Explored
- **Shibuya**: Modern, young, chaotic energy
- **Shinjuku**: Neon lights, Golden Gai bars
- **Harajuku**: Fashion, Takeshita Street crepes
- **Akihabara**: Electronics, anime, retro games
- **Shimokitazawa**: Hip cafes, vintage shops (my favorite!)

## Practical Tips

### Transportation
- JR Pass NOT worth it for just Tokyo
- Suica card is essential
- Google Maps transit is perfect
- Trains stop at midnight (plan accordingly!)

### Language
- Download Google Translate offline
- Most signs have English
- People are incredibly helpful despite language barrier
- Learn basic phrases: arigatou, sumimasen

### Money
- Cash is still king in many places
- 7-Eleven ATMs work with international cards
- Tipping is NOT done (still weird to me)

### Etiquette
- No eating while walking
- Stand on left on escalators (right in Osaka)
- Quiet on trains (everyone whispers)
- Take shoes off when asked

## Would I Do Differently
- Stay longer (2 weeks not enough!)
- Visit in April for cherry blossoms
- Do a day trip to Nikko or Kamakura
- Learn more Japanese beforehand
- Bring extra SD cards for photos
- Try more street food

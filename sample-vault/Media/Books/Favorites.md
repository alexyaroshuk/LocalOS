# Favorite Books

## All-Time Top 10

### 1. Project Hail Mary - Andy Weir
**Genre**: Sci-Fi
**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Why**: Perfect blend of science, humor, and heart. Read it in 2 days!
**Quote**: "I'm pretty much fucked. That's my considered opinion. Fucked."

### 2. Atomic Habits - James Clear
**Genre**: Self-improvement
**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Why**: Actually actionable advice. Changed how I build habits.
**See**: [[Book-Notes/Atomic Habits|Detailed Notes]]

### 3. The Phoenix Project - Gene Kim
**Genre**: Tech/Fiction
**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Why**: DevOps concepts as a thriller. Required reading for tech folks.
**Impact**: Influenced how I think about systems and teams

### 4. Deep Work - Cal Newport
**Genre**: Productivity
**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Why**: Validated my distaste for constant connectivity
**Applied**: Time blocking, focus sessions without phone

### 5. The Martian - Andy Weir
**Genre**: Sci-Fi
**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Why**: Science + problem-solving + humor. Perfect.
**Fun fact**: Read before watching the movie (book is better)

### 6. Sapiens - Yuval Noah Harari
**Genre**: History/Anthropology
**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Why**: Mind-expanding perspective on human history
**Takeaway**: We're all part of shared myths

### 7. Shoe Dog - Phil Knight
**Genre**: Memoir
**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Why**: Raw, honest story of building Nike
**Lesson**: Success is messier than it looks

### 8. The Three-Body Problem - Cixin Liu
**Genre**: Sci-Fi
**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Why**: Unique perspective on first contact
**Note**: Chinese sci-fi is amazing

### 9. Educated - Tara Westover
**Genre**: Memoir
**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Why**: Powerful story of education and self-invention
**Impact**: Made me grateful for my opportunities

### 10. Accelerate - Nicole Forsgren
**Genre**: Tech/DevOps
**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Why**: Data-driven insights on software delivery
**Applied**: At TechCorp for CI/CD improvements
